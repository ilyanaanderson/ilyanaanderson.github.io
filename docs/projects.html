  1. Prediction of Nucleotide Reactivity at Each Position in RNA Molecules Using Transformer Architecture
    Understanding Ribonucleic acid (RNA) molecule structure is crucial in many fields of medicine. Experimental measurements of several types of chemical reactivity can be utilized to derive such structure information. In the present work, I used customized transformer architecture to predict two types of reactivity at each position of RNA molecules as part of the Stanford Ribonanza RNA Folding competition. My best approach yielded 0.15867 mean absolute error (MAE) score on the private leaderboard test data. This solution placed in the top 8% of contest submissions.
