Projects

  1. Prediction of Nucleotide Reactivity at Each Position in RNA Molecules Using Transformer Architecture
    Understanding Ribonucleic acid (RNA) molecule structure is crucial in many fields of medicine. Experimental measurements of several types of chemical reactivity can be utilized to derive such structure information. In the present work, I used customized transformer architecture to predict two types of reactivity at each position of RNA molecules as part of the Stanford Ribonanza RNA Folding competition . My best approach yielded 0.15867 mean absolute error (MAE) score on the private leaderboard test data. This solution placed in the top 8% of contest submissions.

  2. Exploration of Methods to Remove Bias From Models Performing Natural Language Inference Task
    In this project, I make several attempts to remove bias from the models trained for the natural language inference task. I explore the possibility that a model forced to generate a hypothesis based on a premise might be less prone to learning the dataset bias. Another attempt to reduce the learned bias was made by flipping premises and hypotheses for the contradiction examples during the training. Lastly, I implemented the DRiFT method reported in the literature. Most of the enhanced models demonstrated improved performance on the HANS dataset relative to the baseline models, suggesting a potential reduction in bias. Nevertheless, while these results are indicative, they are not conclusive enough to firmly assert the complete removal of bias.
