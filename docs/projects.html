Projects

  1. Prediction of Nucleotide Reactivity at Each Position in RNA Molecules Using Custom Transformer Architecture
    Understanding the ribonucleic acid (RNA) molecule structure is crucial in many fields of medicine. Experimental measurements of several types of chemical reactivity can be utilized to derive such structural information. In the present work, I used a customized transformer architecture to predict two types of reactivity at each position of RNA molecules as part of the Stanford Ribonanza RNA Folding competition on Kaggle. I experimented with rotary and sinusoidal embeddings for encoding positional information, as well as with feature design. My best approach yielded a mean absolute error (MAE) score of 0.15867 on the private leaderboard test data. This solution placed in the top 8% of the contest submissions.

  2. Exploration of Methods to Remove Bias From LLMs Fine-Tuned on Natural Language Inference Task
    In this project, I made several attempts to remove bias from models trained for the natural language inference task, including two novel approaches. I explored the possibility that forcing a model to generate a hypothesis based on a premise might reduce its tendency to learn dataset bias. Another attempt involved flipping premises and hypotheses for contradiction examples during training. Lastly, I implemented the DRiFT method reported in the literature. Most of the enhanced models demonstrated improved performance on the HANS dataset compared to the baseline, suggesting a potential reduction in bias.

  3. Development of a Neural-Network-Based Agent to Play SuperTuxKart Ice Hockey
    The objective of the project was to create an agent capable of playing SuperTuxKart ice hockey by choosing an appropriate action at every turn. Working within a team, we pursued various strategies, including developing a computer vision system from scratch and using reinforcement learning. My focus was on implementing imitation learning and DAGGER algorithms. The team selected my agent design as the basis for further experimentation. This agent placed second in a class-wide competition.
